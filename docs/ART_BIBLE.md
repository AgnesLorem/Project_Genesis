# Art Bible

## Purpose

This document defines the visual direction, asset standards, and review criteria for Project Genesis illustrations.

Project Genesis uses AI-generated illustrations. The purpose of this Art Bible is to make every future AI-generated image look like it belongs to the same game.

This document defines visual style only. It does not approve creature species, evolution stages, gameplay card systems, combat mechanics, rarity systems, cosmetics, or content quantities.

## Status

- Status: Active Draft

- Owner: Art Direction
- Last Updated: TBD

## 1. Art Philosophy

Project Genesis uses an Anime Fantasy art style.

The art direction should feel:

1. Heroic.
2. Magical.
3. Clean.
4. Collectible.
5. Creature-focused.
6. Readable at small sizes.
7. Polished enough for long-term content expansion.

The visual identity should support a creature progression game. Illustrations should make creatures feel worth collecting, improving, and comparing without relying on visual noise or overdesigned effects.

The project should avoid one-off image styles. Every illustration must feel like it came from the same production pipeline, even if generated at different times.

## 2. Core Visual Style

The approved visual style is:

1. Anime fantasy illustration.
2. Clean linework.
3. Polished digital painting.
4. Soft but readable shading.
5. Strong creature silhouette.
6. Controlled magical effects.
7. Vibrant but disciplined color.
8. Characterful expressions and poses.
9. Light stylization rather than photorealism.
10. Game-ready presentation.

Images should look like premium game key art or collectible creature card art, not rough concept sketches.

The target visual quality is finished production illustration. AI outputs that look unfinished, overly abstract, distorted, or inconsistent must be rejected.

## 3. Creature Style

Creatures are the visual center of Project Genesis.

Creature illustrations must follow these rules:

1. The creature must be the dominant subject.
2. The face, head, or primary focal feature must be clear.
3. The body shape must be easy to understand.
4. The pose must communicate personality or combat readiness.
5. The design must be appealing rather than grotesque.
6. The creature must fit anime fantasy rather than realistic wildlife, horror, sci-fi machinery, or abstract monster art.
7. Anatomy may be stylized, but it must feel intentional and stable.
8. Details must support identity, not clutter the design.
9. Magical traits should look integrated into the creature, not pasted on.
10. Creature visuals must avoid implying unapproved mechanics.

Creature designs should be expressive and collectible. They may be cute, elegant, fierce, mystical, or powerful, but they must remain within the same anime fantasy universe.

Creature illustrations must not include UI text, stats, rarity labels, numbers, watermarks, signatures, logos, or unrelated characters.

## 4. Evolution Style

Evolution art must show continuity and growth.

If a creature has multiple visual forms, each form should appear related to the same identity through:

1. Shared color family.
2. Shared primary silhouette motif.
3. Shared face or head language.
4. Shared magical theme.
5. Shared material or texture language.
6. Clear increase in presence or refinement.

Evolution should feel like progression, not replacement.

Earlier forms should read as simpler and more approachable. Later forms should read as more powerful, refined, or majestic while preserving recognizable design DNA.

Evolution art must not imply unapproved branching evolution, rarity tiers, stat changes, abilities, or gameplay requirements. Those rules belong in design and data documents.

## 5. Color Palette

Project Genesis uses a vivid anime fantasy palette with controlled contrast.

### Primary Palette Direction

1. Jewel-toned fantasy colors.
2. Clean blues, teals, violets, emeralds, warm golds, and controlled rose accents.
3. Soft atmospheric neutrals for backgrounds.
4. High readability between creature and background.
5. Saturation focused on the creature, not the entire image.

### Recommended Color Roles

| Role | Direction | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Primary creature color | Saturated fantasy hue | Main identity color for the creature. |
| Secondary creature color | Harmonized adjacent or complementary hue | Supports markings, wings, armor-like shapes, or accents. |
| Magical accent | Bright controlled highlight | Used sparingly for eyes, runes, glow, particles, or energy. |
| Shadow color | Cool violet, blue, or muted neutral | Keeps anime fantasy mood without muddying the subject. |
| Highlight color | Warm gold, pale cyan, soft pink, or clean white | Used for rim light, focal points, and premium finish. |
| Background color | Lower saturation atmospheric color | Supports subject without competing. |

### Palette Rules

1. Do not use muddy brown-heavy palettes as the default.
2. Do not let the whole image become one dominant hue.
3. Do not use neon saturation across the full image.
4. Do not use dark, low-contrast palettes that obscure the creature.
5. Do not use realistic animal coloration if it breaks the anime fantasy identity.
6. Accent colors should guide the eye, not overwhelm the design.

## 6. Silhouette Rules

Silhouette readability is mandatory.

Every creature illustration must pass these rules:

1. The creature must be recognizable as a single subject at small size.
2. The outer shape must not dissolve into background effects.
3. The head or primary focal feature must be identifiable.
4. Limbs, wings, tails, horns, or other extensions must not create unreadable tangles.
5. Magical effects must not obscure the creature outline.
6. The silhouette must avoid excessive spikes, ribbons, particles, or floating fragments.
7. The pose must avoid hiding the body behind foreground effects.
8. The creature should remain readable in grayscale.
9. The design should have one or two strong silhouette hooks, not many competing hooks.
10. Evolution forms should retain a recognizable silhouette relationship.

Rejected silhouettes include:

1. Blobby shapes with no focal identity.
2. Overly complex shapes that collapse at thumbnail size.
3. Human-like poses unless the creature design clearly requires it.
4. Shapes that look like unrelated genre assets.

## 7. Composition

Illustrations should be composed for game UI reuse.

Composition rules:

1. Use a clear central subject.
2. Prefer three-quarter view for creature readability.
3. Keep the subject large enough to read in thumbnails.
4. Leave safe margins around the subject.
5. Avoid cutting off important creature features.
6. Avoid extreme closeups unless specifically approved.
7. Avoid distant full-scene shots where the creature becomes secondary.
8. Use a clean focal hierarchy: face first, body second, effects third, background last.
9. Keep the action pose readable.
10. Use diagonal energy sparingly to create motion without clutter.

Default framing:

1. Creature occupies roughly 60-75 percent of image height.
2. Face or focal feature sits near the upper-middle third.
3. Feet, tail, wings, or extensions should remain inside safe crop unless intentionally framed.
4. Background should support depth but not tell a separate story.

## 8. Lighting

Lighting should create a premium anime fantasy finish.

Lighting rules:

1. Use clear key light.
2. Use soft fill light to preserve readable forms.
3. Use controlled rim light to separate creature from background.
4. Use magical glow only where it supports the creature identity.
5. Keep eyes and focal features readable.
6. Avoid harsh horror lighting.
7. Avoid flat unlit rendering.
8. Avoid overexposed bloom.
9. Avoid noisy particle glow.
10. Maintain consistent light direction within each image.

Preferred lighting mood:

1. Bright heroic fantasy.
2. Soft cinematic glow.
3. Clean atmospheric depth.
4. Polished collectible finish.

Lighting should make the creature feel valuable and readable, not obscure or chaotic.

## 9. Background

Backgrounds should support the creature without competing with it.

Background rules:

1. Use fantasy-inspired environments or abstract magical atmospheres.
2. Keep background detail lower than creature detail.
3. Use softer edges in the background than on the creature.
4. Avoid strong background shapes that merge with the creature silhouette.
5. Avoid text, symbols, UI, logos, or readable signage.
6. Avoid unrelated characters or creatures.
7. Avoid modern city, sci-fi, military, or realistic documentary environments.
8. Background color must contrast with the creature enough for card and UI readability.
9. Background should imply world mood without approving specific world content.
10. Backgrounds must not imply unapproved mechanics or locations.

Acceptable background directions:

1. Soft magical forest atmosphere.
2. Floating light particles.
3. Fantasy sky glow.
4. Abstract elemental aura.
5. Soft ruins or natural shapes without gameplay-specific meaning.

These examples are visual directions only and do not approve world zones, maps, or creature habitats.

## 10. Card Illustration Rules

Card illustration means a reusable framed illustration format for UI or collection presentation. This section does not approve a gameplay card system.

Card illustration rules:

1. No text inside the generated illustration.
2. No fake UI frames inside the generated illustration unless specifically requested by UI direction.
3. Subject must remain readable if cropped into a card frame.
4. Keep important features inside a central safe area.
5. Leave enough edge room for future UI framing.
6. Avoid important details in extreme corners.
7. Use vertical composition when the asset is intended for portrait cards.
8. Use square composition when the asset is intended for icons or collection tiles.
9. Background must remain lower contrast than the creature.
10. Effects must not cover future UI label areas.
11. Creature identity must be clear at thumbnail size.
12. The image must not include rarity stars, stat numbers, badges, labels, or buttons.

Recommended card prompt phrase:

```text
anime fantasy creature illustration, clean collectible game art, centered subject, readable silhouette, polished digital painting, soft magical background, no text, no UI
```

## 11. AI Prompt Structure

All AI image prompts should use a consistent structure.

Recommended structure:

```text
[subject description], anime fantasy creature illustration, Project Genesis style, clean linework, polished digital painting, vivid controlled colors, readable silhouette, three-quarter view, expressive pose, soft magical lighting, subtle fantasy background, collectible game art, high detail on subject, low detail background, no text, no UI
```

Prompt components:

1. Subject description: what the creature or illustration is.
2. Style lock: anime fantasy creature illustration.
3. Project identity: Project Genesis style.
4. Rendering: clean linework and polished digital painting.
5. Color: vivid controlled colors.
6. Readability: readable silhouette and clear focal point.
7. Pose: expressive but not cluttered.
8. Lighting: soft magical lighting.
9. Background: subtle fantasy background.
10. Asset purpose: collectible game art.
11. Exclusions: no text and no UI.

Prompt writers must avoid adding gameplay mechanics, rarity labels, exact stats, named worlds, or unapproved evolution rules into image prompts.

## 12. Negative Prompt Rules

Every AI image generation request should include negative prompt guidance.

Standard negative prompt:

```text
photorealistic, realistic animal photography, western comic style, gritty horror, gore, body horror, sci-fi armor, mecha, guns, modern clothing, modern city, low quality, blurry, noisy, muddy colors, overexposed, underexposed, bad anatomy, extra limbs, missing limbs, distorted face, unreadable silhouette, cluttered background, excessive particles, text, logo, watermark, signature, UI, frame, stat numbers, rarity stars
```

Negative prompt rules:

1. Always exclude text, logos, watermarks, and signatures.
2. Always exclude UI unless the task is explicitly UI art.
3. Exclude photorealism.
4. Exclude horror, gore, and body horror.
5. Exclude sci-fi machinery unless future art direction approves it.
6. Exclude modern weapons and modern clothing.
7. Exclude muddy colors and unreadable silhouettes.
8. Exclude excessive particles and overdesigned effects.
9. Exclude distorted anatomy and AI artifact patterns.
10. Exclude references to living artists, copyrighted characters, or another game's exact style.

Negative prompts must be adjusted when a specific asset type needs additional exclusions.

## 13. Consistency Rules

Consistency is more important than novelty.

All generated illustrations must follow these rules:

1. Use anime fantasy as the base style.
2. Use the same polish level across creatures.
3. Keep linework clean and controlled.
4. Keep lighting soft, magical, and readable.
5. Keep backgrounds supportive and lower priority than subjects.
6. Keep creature anatomy stylized but coherent.
7. Keep visual complexity appropriate for thumbnail readability.
8. Preserve color harmony across related forms.
9. Preserve silhouette identity across evolution-related forms.
10. Use stable naming and visual references when regenerating a creature.
11. Store approved prompt text with final asset notes when asset tracking exists.
12. Record rejected prompt patterns when they produce off-style results.

Batch generation rules:

1. Generate related creature sets with shared style instructions.
2. Review all images together for palette, rendering, and silhouette consistency.
3. Reject images that look like they belong to a different franchise or genre.
4. Do not approve a single impressive image if it breaks the shared style.

## 14. Forbidden Styles

The following styles are forbidden for Project Genesis unless project leadership explicitly revises the Art Bible:

1. Photorealism.
2. Realistic wildlife photography.
3. Gritty dark fantasy realism.
4. Horror creature design.
5. Gore or body horror.
6. Western superhero comic rendering.
7. Heavy painterly oil portrait style.
8. Low-poly 3D render style.
9. Plastic toy render style.
10. Pixel art.
11. Chibi-only proportions as the default style.
12. Mecha or hard sci-fi creature design.
13. Military tactical design.
14. Cyberpunk city style.
15. Minimal flat vector style.
16. Sketch-only concept art as final art.
17. Overly abstract magical shapes with no readable creature identity.
18. Direct imitation of existing games, anime, franchises, characters, or living artists.

Forbidden does not mean the project can never explore a visual idea. It means the idea is not part of the approved baseline and cannot be used without documented approval.

## 15. Quality Checklist

Every AI-generated illustration must pass this checklist before approval:

1. The image is clearly anime fantasy.
2. The image looks like finished game art, not a rough concept.
3. The creature is the dominant subject.
4. The silhouette is readable at thumbnail size.
5. The face or primary focal feature is clear.
6. Anatomy is coherent and free of obvious AI distortion.
7. The pose is expressive but readable.
8. Colors are vibrant but controlled.
9. The palette fits Project Genesis.
10. Lighting separates the creature from the background.
11. Magical effects support the creature instead of covering it.
12. Background detail does not compete with the subject.
13. No text, watermark, logo, signature, stat number, or fake UI is present.
14. The image does not imply unapproved mechanics.
15. The image does not imitate another franchise or named artist.
16. The image can be cropped safely for UI presentation.
17. The image is consistent with related creature or evolution art.
18. The image avoids forbidden styles.
19. The prompt and negative prompt can be reused or audited.
20. The asset is approved only after review against this Art Bible.

If an image fails any checklist item, it must be revised, regenerated, or rejected.
